Ball Club Salary Analysis

(2000 - 2019)

We sat out to answer the question, Does Money Win Championships? How does money correlate to Wins? Is there a relationship between the two, and if so, by how much? First, we began by loading in salary data upon the start of a season and track how many wins a ball club notched during the remainder of the regular season. From there we made calculations based on percentage difference from previous season and the rise and fall of salary and wins. Additionally, we used Tableau's preloaded 'Moving Average' functions to see the relationship between our two metrics. Diving into the leagues and divisions we noticed two outliers in the National League Central and American League East divisions that raised some more quesstions that we delved into.

By using the linear regression models, we were able to see that there was an ever-so-slight positive correlation between a team's increase in salary numbers to an increase in the number of wins. With an R value approaching 0.1, there is not enough direct evidence to ensure that throwing money at a team ensures success. There are a myriad of factors that go into a team's ability to be successful at the professional level as one would assume.
