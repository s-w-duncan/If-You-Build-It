Pitching and Player Salary Analysis

(2011 - 2015)

When evaluating earned run average (ERA) and starting pitcher salary, an individual would assume that a high salary associates to a lower ERA. There is not a consistent correlation to salary and starting pitcher performance. Players with the lowest salary per innings pitched on the team would assumptively have a higher ERA. A “good” ERA performance averages around 3.0, a lower pitcher ERA associates to valuable pitcher performance. In this model the pitcher with the lowest ERA is also paid the lowest salary per inning. ERA does not necessarily guarantee a high pitcher salary. In the final visualizations, there is a display of each teams ERA average over the years. ERA’s are not necessarily consistent due to variables such as pitcher, batter and fielding player abilities.
